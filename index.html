THE WALL

Every other tool asks you to arrive organized.

The blank project. The empty box. The setup screen that wants categories, tags, and a plan before you've even started — most “ADHD-friendly” tools just paint softer colors onto the same machine.

For an ADHD brain, that first step is the wall. So the thought stays stuck in your head, the tab stays open forever, and the guilt just quietly piles up.

WHAT GOVERNS EVERY SCREEN

Calm is a design decision.

Five principles. They hold on every page, or they don't ship.
01 Loudness gradient Editorial volume scales to emotion. The rescue room is the quietest place in the product; the project list is the loudest. Nothing shouts unless it has earned it.
02 Re-entry over status Lead with what you last touched and the one next step — never vanity stats. No percent-done, no streaks, no scoreboard of everything you didn't get to.
03 One focus at a time Lists cap at about five, then quietly fold. The single next action is always the most prominent thing on the screen. Everything else waits its turn.
04 Input is always home base The "just talk" bar is right there on every screen. However lost you feel, you can always bail back to a plain conversation and start from your own words.
05 One human line per page Exactly one line of gold Newsreader italic — the emotional anchor. It talks to you like a person, not a system. One per page, and it never tries too hard.
